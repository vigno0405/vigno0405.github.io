About

My interests lie in the integration of compliant control and machine learning for the design, modeling, and control of soft and continuum mechanical systems, with a particular focus on bio-inspired robotics where rigid-body paradigms are limited. I am especially drawn to embodied intelligence and impedance-shaped interaction, exploring how the natural dynamics of compliant bodies can be harnessed instead of canceled, to achieve dexterous manipulation and adaptive locomotion. My goal is to develop principled, data-driven frameworks for perception and control that enable robust and versatile behavior in contact-rich, dynamic environments.

Research

Compliant Control and Optimization

Virtual Model Control (VMC) and energy-tank passivity for safe, stiffness-modulating interaction. Hybrid architectures combine analytical models (Piecewise Constant Curvature Jacobians) with learning-based inverse kinematics and gradient-descent tuning for redundancy resolution.

Dexterous Manipulation and Soft Continuum Arms

Tendon-driven anthropomorphic hands (ADAPT) with programmable passivity, decoupling grip force from grip stiffness for tasks ranging from piano playing to surgical needle insertion. Continuum manipulators (Trimmed Helicoid) use VMC for whole-body shaping and obstacle avoidance, with vision-based proprioception via embedded miniature cameras and CNN pose estimation.

Bio-Inspired Locomotion

Multi-modal swimming with two-DOF tendon-driven tails (RoboFish) that transition between anguilliform, carangiform, and thunniform gaits by modulating body impedance. Turtle-inspired soft crawlers built on Variable Stiffness Actuators and open-loop Central Pattern Generators, characterized across flat, granular, and sloped terrains.
